I am a **Postdoctoral Researcher** at the Chair of Information Systems | Software & AI Business at the Technical University of Darmstadt.

My research explores how artificial intelligence is reshaping human learning at the team, organizational, and societal levels. I study how people acquire, retain, and share knowledge in interaction with AI: when AI supports or constrains learning and collective knowledge creation, and how organizations can design human–AI collaboration to foster innovation.

I investigate these questions through a combination of computer simulations, laboratory experiments, and software prototypes, with a broader interest in AI systems that work *with* people rather than simply replacing them.

## Research Highlights

| 🤝 Exploring Collaboration in Human–AI Teams: A Design Science Approach to Team–AI Collaboration Systems                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| <div align="center">**PACIS 2024** · Completed Research Paper</div>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| We designed and evaluated a Discord-based platform where AI joins human teams as a configurable colleague with assignable roles and personalities. Configurable AI helped teams deliberate more effectively, but human decision-makers still tended to favor human judgment over AI suggestions when it mattered most.  |
| **Programmed Solution:**<br>• Team–AI collaboration system built inside Discord (using discord.py and the OpenAI API)<br>• GPT-4 Turbo powered AI agents with configurable roles and personalities<br>• MySQL backend for interaction logging and experimental analysis                                                 |
| **Authors:** Patrick Hendriks · Timo Sturm · Maximilian Geis · Till Grimminger · Benedikt Mast                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| 🔗 [Read on ResearchGate][1]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |

<br>

| 🤖 Organizational Learning and Enactment in the Era of Artificial Intelligence                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| <div align="center">**ICIS 2024** · Completed Research Paper</div>                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| We used agent-based simulation to show that AI helps organizations perform well regardless of how their members prefer to learn. However, as AI shapes decisions, it gradually shifts what the organization comes to treat as "truth."                                                      |
| **Programmed Solution:**<br>• Agent-based simulation extending Miller and Lin's (2010) organizational learning and enactment model<br>• Human agents with three learning preferences and four conditions of decision autonomy<br>• 3 million simulation runs on TU Darmstadt's HPC cluster  |
| **Authors:** Patrick Hendriks · Timo Sturm · Christian Olt · Ning Nan · Peter Buxmann                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| 🔗 [Read on ResearchGate][2]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |

<br>

| 🌍 The Impact of Artificial Intelligence on the Evolution of Culture                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| <div align="center">**ICIS 2024** · Completed Research Paper</div>                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| We used agent-based simulation to show that localized or "national" AI does not reliably preserve cultural diversity and can in fact accelerate cultural convergence among neighboring groups. More globalized AI increases the number of distinct cultural groups, but at the cost of greater polarization.                                                               |
| **Programmed Solution:**<br>• Agent-based simulation extending Axelrod's (1997) cultural evolution model to include AI as a cultural learning actor<br>• AI agents with varying degrees of cultural alignment and varying levels of social influence on humans<br>• 15,000 simulation runs on TU Darmstadt's HPC cluster, each simulating 51.2 million agent interactions  |
| **Authors:** Patrick Hendriks · Timo Sturm · Maren Mehler · Peter Buxmann                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 🔗 [Read on ResearchGate][3]                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
